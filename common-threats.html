Common Online Threats

What Is a Cyber Threat?

Cyber threats are malicious activities that target computers, networks, and digital systems to steal data, disrupt operations, or cause damage. As individuals and organizations rely more on the internet, the risk of cyber attacks continues to grow.

These threats can come from hackers, scammers, or even automated programs designed to exploit weaknesses in software or human behavior.

Phishing

Phishing is when attackers impersonate trusted people or organizations to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or bank details.

  • Fake emails from banks or companies
  • Links that lead to fake login pages
  • Urgent messages asking for action

Malware

Malware is malicious software designed to damage systems or steal information. It can infect your device through downloads, email attachments, or unsafe websites.

  • Viruses and worms
  • Ransomware (locks your files)
  • Spyware (tracks your activity)

Identity Theft

Identity theft occurs when someone steals your personal information to commit fraud, such as opening accounts or making purchases in your name.

  • Stolen social security numbers
  • Unauthorized bank transactions
  • Fake accounts created in your name

Ransomware

Ransomware locks your files or system and demands payment to restore access. Paying does not guarantee recovery.

Social Engineering

This type of attack manipulates people into giving up confidential information by pretending to be trustworthy.

Data Breaches

A data breach happens when sensitive data is exposed due to weak security, often affecting large numbers of users.

Real-World Example

Many phishing attacks look like legitimate emails from companies like Amazon, banks, or schools. They often include logos and formatting that appear real but contain fake links.

Always double-check the sender and avoid clicking suspicious links.
