Safe Email and Web Use

Email and web browsing are common ways cybercriminals try to access your personal information. Learning how to recognize suspicious messages and unsafe websites is one of the most important steps in staying safe online.

Safe Email Practices

  • Verify the sender’s email address carefully
  • Be cautious of urgent or threatening language
  • Do not click unknown or shortened links
  • Do not download unexpected attachments
  • Hover over links to preview the real URL

Unknown Sender

Emails from unfamiliar addresses or slightly altered company names are often scams.

Urgent Messages

Scammers try to rush you into acting quickly without thinking.

Spelling Errors

Many phishing emails contain grammar mistakes or unusual wording.

Requests for Info

Legitimate companies will never ask for passwords or personal data via email.

Strange Links

Links that don’t match the official website are a major red flag.

Unexpected Attachments

Attachments may contain malware or viruses that infect your device.

Safe Web Browsing

When browsing the internet, always make sure the website is secure and trustworthy before entering any personal information.

  • Look for HTTPS in the URL
  • Avoid suspicious or unfamiliar websites
  • Do not enter personal info on unsecured pages
  • Keep your browser updated

Quick Safety Tips

  • ✔ Never share personal information through email
  • ✔ Double-check website URLs before logging in
  • ✔ Use spam filters to block suspicious emails
  • ✔ Delete suspicious messages immediately
  • ✔ Report phishing emails when possible
